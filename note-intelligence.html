Gradient Descent is an optimization algorithm that minimizes a loss function by iteratively moving in the direction of steepest descent. The learning rate controls each step's size — too high causes overshooting, too low slows convergence.

Three variants exist: SGD (one sample at a time), Mini-batch (small groups), and Full Batch (all data). Momentum helps escape local minima, while Adam combines adaptive learning rates with momentum for best-in-class performance.
Q: What does the learning rate control in gradient descent?
A: The size of each step taken toward the minimum of the loss function.
Q: What is the key difference between SGD and full-batch gradient descent?
A: SGD updates weights after each sample; full-batch uses the entire dataset per update.
Q: What two techniques does the Adam optimizer combine?
A: Adaptive learning rates + momentum.
